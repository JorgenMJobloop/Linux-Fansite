The Linux Kernel

Linux is built by Linus Torvalds. The "Linux-kernel" is a alternative kernel built on UNIX. And is nowadays a valid competitor to Apple's UNIX-based XNU-Kernel. And Microsoft's NT-Kernel. One of the Linux-kernel's greatest strenghts, is the freedom each developer have, to implement it and built full systems and operating systems, upon it's technology, and thereby creating a more diverse technology marked, where each user has a right to choose.

The Linux Foundation
From wikipedia:The Linux Foundation (LF) is a non-profit technology consortium founded in 2000 as a merger between Open Source Development Labs and the Free Standards Group to standardize Linux, support its growth, and promote its commercial adoption. Additionally, it hosts and promotes the collaborative development of open source software projects.[2][3][4] The foundation was launched in 2000, under the Open Source Development Labs (OSDL) and became the organization it is today when OSDL merged with the Free Standards Group (FSG). The Linux Foundation sponsors the work of Linux creator Linus Torvalds and lead maintainer Greg Kroah-Hartman. Furthermore, it is supported by members, such as AT&T, Cisco, Fujitsu, Google, Hitachi, Huawei, IBM, Intel, Meta,[5] Microsoft,[6] NEC, Oracle, Orange S.A., Qualcomm, Samsung,[7] Tencent, and VMware, as well as developers from around the world.
